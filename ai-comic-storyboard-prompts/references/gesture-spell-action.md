# Gesture And Spell Action Knowledge

Use this knowledge base when the scene includes hand seals, finger signs, sword-finger casting, spell gestures, elemental techniques, summoning, clone creation, transformation, formations, or xianxia ability release.

This file is a functional extraction and classification of gesture-action grammar for storyboard generation.

## Core Principle

Gesture actions must be readable as choreography, not vague glowing poses.

Every gesture shot should describe:

- Hand gesture name: sword-finger seal, cross seal, lotus seal, triangular tower seal, palm seal, sleeve wave, clenched-fist seal.
- Finger structure: which fingers extend, touch, bend, cross, tighten, or separate.
- Wrist and arm route: push forward, unfold outward, draw a circle, rotate inward, drop suddenly, lift to chest, sweep sleeve.
- Camera lock point: fingertips, knuckles, wrist crossing point, palm center, sleeve edge, seal center, eye-to-finger axis.
- Energy source: fingertip particles, palm array, wrist ring, sleeve airflow, eye light, ground formation.
- Motion trace: sword-qi line, circular magic trail, petal spiral, water-ring arc, fire-dragon pattern, leaf vortex.
- Physical feedback: sleeve snap, hair lift, ground dust, rain suspension, air pressure ring, particles dragged by acceleration.
- Release or closure result: thrust, burst, formation completion, transformation, clone smoke, pressure diffusion, energy collapse.

If the prompt only says "结印", "掐诀", "施法", "法术爆发", or "灵力释放", expand it into the full chain above.

## Gesture Library

### Sword-Finger Seal / 剑指诀

- Finger structure: index and middle fingers extend together as one blade line; thumb presses the bent ring and little fingers; knuckles are visibly tense when power rises.
- Best use: sword qi, talisman line, light blade, directional spell, precision strike.
- Camera grammar: macro hand close-up first; 45-degree side orbit with micro push; then rapid push-in following the fingertips as the spell fires.
- Visual route: gold or cold-white particles gather at the fingertip seam, stretch into a thin bright line, then become a sword-qi arc or magic-circle stroke.
- Body feedback: wide sleeve snaps backward, wrist cloth tightens, shoulders drop, hair tips lift from air pressure.
- Good release verbs: thrust forward, carve across air, pierce through mist, draw a circle, slash down by fingertip command.

### Cross Seal / 十字诀

- Finger structure: both hands cross at the wrists or palms; fingers form opposing blade-like lines; fingertips remain sharp and symmetrical.
- Best use: barrier opening, clone activation, summoning, petal or talisman array.
- Camera grammar: vertical close-up from front, slight pullback, then orbit around the crossed wrists.
- Visual route: particles gather at the intersection point, then the hands unfold left and right to draw a full circular seal before returning to the cross.
- Feedback: sleeves pull into opposite arcs, petals or sparks are dragged into a ring, the background briefly compresses toward the wrist center.
- Closure: hands snap back into the cross seal; the circle locks; the formation flashes or releases.

### Lotus Seal / 莲花起势

- Finger structure: both hands held before chest, fingers relaxed but precise, fingertips lightly touching or opening like petals.
- Best use: elegant transformation, healing, illusion, charm-free ethereal spell, calm-before-burst xianxia casting.
- Camera grammar: fixed frontal close-up with slow micro push; do not smear the calm phase.
- Visual route: fine particles rise without long drag lines; light gathers in layered petal shapes around the fingertips.
- Beat design: keep the first seconds slow and clear, then switch to a sharp eye-open or hand-drop ignition.
- Closure: the hands lower or open; the body aura expands; the new form, outfit state, or environment completes in one decisive burst.

### Triangular Tower Seal / 三角尖塔印

- Finger structure: both hands combine into a triangular or spire-like shape; fingertips create a clear top point.
- Best use: high-altitude sword formation, sky array, large-area spell, divine-pressure build-up.
- Camera grammar: low-angle micro push toward the seal; then half-orbit around upper body and hands; end on a wide reveal.
- Visual route: formation lines climb from the triangular point, spreading into layered sword seals or geometric arrays above the character.
- Feedback: robes and sleeves pull upward; clouds, rain, or sword shadows bend toward the seal center.
- Closure: formation completes with a clear lock-frame before the attack descends.

### Palm Seal / 掌印

- Finger structure: open palm, overlapped palms, or one palm pressing over the other; palm center must be visible when the spell source matters.
- Best use: water wall, fire wave, energy push, barrier, shockwave, pressure release.
- Camera grammar: push from palm center toward the target line; if impact is huge, cut to a front-facing compression shot before release.
- Visual route: array appears in palm center, expands to wrist, then projects outward as a disc, wave, ring, or beam.
- Feedback: sleeve edge ripples first, then hair and surrounding dust/rain respond.
- Closure: palm stops sharply; energy travels after the stop, creating delayed force.

### Sleeve Wave / 挥袖诀

- Finger structure: fingers may be hidden, but the wrist line and sleeve edge must be readable.
- Best use: water wave, petal storm, fog sweep, clone smoke, clearing the battlefield.
- Camera grammar: follow the sleeve edge as the main moving blade; lock focus at the sleeve tip during the sweep.
- Visual route: particles start inside the sleeve shadow, spill out at the cuff, then expand into wave, mist, petals, or smoke.
- Feedback: sleeve fabric stretches, folds snap, nearby particles are pulled into a curved wake.
- Closure: sleeve stops; the element continues moving and hits or surrounds the target.

### Clenched-Fist Seal / 握拳收束

- Finger structure: open fingers close one by one or snap shut together; knuckles whiten or glow; wrist tendons tighten.
- Best use: spell compression, pressure lock, finishing trigger, ground energy detonation.
- Camera grammar: fast pullback or upward pull as the fist closes; freeze for a fraction at the final clench.
- Visual route: loose particles reverse into the fist, then burst outward as a pressure ring or ground diffusion.
- Feedback: air compresses, cloth at the wrist tightens, ground particles rise before the shock travels.
- Closure: fist locks; energy detonates elsewhere, so the cause-and-effect chain is visible.

## Five-Second Gesture Modules

### Precision Sword-Qi Release

- 0.0-1.0s: macro side close-up; right hand rises before chest; index and middle fingers extend together; thumb locks the bent fingers; fine gold particles gather between the two extended fingers.
- 1.0-2.0s: the sword-finger thrusts forward; camera rushes with the fingertips; sleeve snaps backward; a thin sword-qi line stretches from the fingertip seam.
- 2.0-3.5s: 360-degree orbit follows the moving hand; the fingers carve a circle in air; golden circle trails remain as clean magic strokes.
- 3.5-4.0s: fast pullback and upward freeze; the hand closes into a fist; particles reverse into the knuckles.
- 4.0-5.0s: low-angle settle; the fist radiates pressure downward; ground light spreads as a circular diffusion.

### Calm-To-Burst Transformation

- 0.0-3.0s: fixed frontal close-up; both hands form a lotus seal before the chest; particles float upward clearly without smear; camera makes a slow micro push.
- 3.0-4.5s: eyes open or head angle changes; one hand drops sharply; light flow bursts from the hand route; camera locks face and hands in a fast push.
- 4.5-7.0s: 360-degree orbit reveals the completed transformed state and changed environment; particles shift from scattered points to stable aura layers.

### Cross-Seal Formation

- 0.0-1.0s: vertical close-up; crossed wrists fill the frame; camera pulls back slightly and begins orbit.
- 1.0-3.0s: both hands unfold outward, draw a complete circle, and return to the cross; particles follow the fingertip arc.
- 3.0-4.5s: rapid push; fingertips brighten; palm or wrist center becomes the transition focus.
- 4.5-5.0s: the final double-hand seal freezes; camera pops back and continues a short orbit; particles flare at maximum brightness.

### High-Air Sword Formation

- 0.0-2.0s: low-angle micro push; character suspended or standing above terrain; triangular tower seal forms before the upper body.
- 2.0-5.0s: low-angle rapid push with half-orbit; hands combine into sword-point command; multiple sword seals open across the sky.
- 5.0-6.0s: slow pull to a wide freeze; sword shadows, formation rings, and cloud pressure clarify scale before the release.

## Element Gesture Routes

### Water

- Gesture source: palm seal, sleeve wave, crossed-wrist seal, or sword-finger drawing a water ring.
- Visual signs: suspended rain, droplets hovering like beads, blue water array in palm center, rippling rings, wave wall, foam edge.
- Camera: overhead or low-angle wide reveal after a clear hand close-up; follow the water edge when it becomes the main moving object.
- Feedback: wet sleeve edge darkens, rain bends toward the seal, ground puddles ripple before the attack moves.
- Avoid: random blue glow with no droplet route or no hand-to-water cause.

### Fire

- Gesture source: flipping hand seals, palm ignition, triangular seal, sleeve wave.
- Visual signs: dark-gold fire patterns, seal sparks, heat shimmer, fire-dragon curve, burning formation lines, sky fire sea.
- Camera: low-angle micro push during seal formation; rapid push or half-orbit as fire patterns connect; wide reveal for large formation.
- Feedback: air distorts around fingertips first; sleeve edges lift from heat; background colors pulse warm only at ignition beats.
- Avoid: continuous full-frame flame without readable finger or formation origin.

### Wood / Leaf / Bamboo

- Gesture source: hand seal before upper body, sleeve wave through bamboo or leaves, palm downward growth command.
- Visual signs: leaf vortex, green shock ring, bamboo bending in sequence, vine-like light lines, feather or bird-shaped transformation effect.
- Camera: vertical overhead push through leaves or bamboo; stop-push to the hand seal; reverse pull to reveal full-body scale.
- Feedback: leaves move in layers from near to far; bamboo bends with delayed rhythm; ground dust lifts in a green-tinted ring.
- Avoid: static green aura with no plant movement or no environmental response.

### Gold / Light / Sword

- Gesture source: sword-finger seal, clenched-fist seal, triangular tower seal.
- Visual signs: gold particles, cold-white blade line, fingertip magic circle, sword shadows, light arc, pressure ring.
- Camera: hand macro first, then finger-following rush, then orbit or wide formation reveal.
- Feedback: sleeve snap and fingertip afterimage should precede the light arc; particles drag only during the fast phase.
- Avoid: making the whole frame equally bright; preserve the fingertip, impact point, and formation structure.

### Flower / Petal / Illusion

- Gesture source: lotus seal, cross seal, sleeve wave, two-hand circle.
- Visual signs: pink or pale particles, petals drawn into a ring, soft but directional trails, petal burst at the seal lock.
- Camera: gentle close-up during formation, then controlled orbit; use a sharp lock-frame at the completion beat.
- Feedback: petals should react to wrist motion and sleeve wake; background may shift only after gesture completion.
- Avoid: purely decorative petal rain unrelated to hand motion.

## Camera Routes For Hand Techniques

- Hand-seal close-up to core-action lock: start on fingertips and knuckles; lock focus until the gesture meaning is readable.
- Low-mid angle micro push to double-hand seal: use when power is gathering at chest height.
- 360-degree orbit plus sudden push: use when the seal completes and the spell needs a pressure spike.
- Constant-distance orbit around particles: use when the hand draws a magic circle or gathers energy around a fixed center.
- Vertical overhead acceleration down to slow stop: use for formations, rain, bamboo, or battlefield-scale spell activation.
- Sleeve-edge follow: use when the sleeve or cuff becomes the leading motion object.
- Finger-following rush: use for sword-finger release, talisman line, projectile, or precision command.
- Pullback freeze: use when the spell result must be revealed after the gesture.

## Gesture Action Quality Rules

- The audience must see where the power comes from before the spell fires.
- Slow build-up phases must stay clear; do not add blur before the gesture structure is understood.
- Blur appears only during the fast release, snap, thrust, sleeve sweep, or impact beat.
- For big xianxia techniques, separate the phases: hand close-up, seal route, formation reveal, release, environmental feedback.
- The hand should not float independently from the body. Shoulder drop, elbow angle, wrist tension, sleeve pull, and stance must support the gesture.
- Every spell needs a cause-and-effect chain: finger shape -> energy gather -> trace or array -> release -> target or environment reaction.
- If a shot contains clones or summons, include the exact triggering gesture and smoke or particle timing.
- If transformation is present, make the gesture complete before the body or environment changes.

## Safety Adaptation

- Keep gesture and impact descriptions cinematic and non-graphic.
- Replace graphic physical harm with light burst, smoke collapse, armor shock, energy overload, dust plume, weapon spark, silhouette displacement, or particle disintegration.
- Avoid eroticized body focus in transformation or character close-ups. Describe costume, silhouette, aura, posture, expression, and motion instead.
- Do not use on-screen captions, subtitles, watermarks, or visible text unless the user explicitly requests a UI/HUD element.

## Required Additions Per Storyboard Shot

When a shot uses spell gestures, add these details inside the foreground, midground, or action line:

- Gesture baseline: exact finger shape and wrist relation at this moment.
- Energy baseline: where particles, array lines, or element flow begin.
- Motion route: what path the hand or sleeve travels during this shot.
- Camera lock: what the camera tracks and when it changes focus.
- Feedback: cloth, hair, rain, dust, leaves, sparks, ground, or target response.

Example compact line:

`[前景]：微距侧仰拍锁住右手剑指，食指与中指并拢前伸，拇指压住弯曲的无名指与小指，虎口布料绷紧；镜头随指尖急推，金色粒子从两指缝拉成一道锋利剑气线，宽袖被反向气压猛然掀起。`
