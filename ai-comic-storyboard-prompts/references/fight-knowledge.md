# Fight and S-Level Action Knowledge Base

Use this reference when the script includes fighting, threat, chase, martial arts, weapons, monsters, demons, skill release, transformation, or pre-fight pressure.

Do not apply this reference to pure dialogue drama. If the source only contains confrontation, intimidation, grief, negotiation, confession, investigation, or threat without sustained attack-defense, keep it as `文戏` and use 2-3 second cut-based drama pacing. One-take action grammar is forbidden in 文戏 unless the user explicitly asks for it.

Before designing action, classify the action type:

- `传统武打`: grounded fists/palms/weapons/footwork, with readable stance, force path, contact point, recoil, opponent reaction, and aftermath.
- `修仙打戏`: supernatural cultivation/fantasy combat, with pressure field, qi/energy gathering, spell/sword-energy release, collision/interruption, environmental response, particle aftermath, and character reaction.
- `混合转场`: dialogue pressure plus a physical or supernatural burst. Split into separate 文戏 and 打戏 units whenever possible; if one 15-second unit must contain both, apply this reference only to the action rows.

## 15-Second Big Storyboard Rule

Every major action module should be treated as a 15-second big storyboard unless the user requests another duration.

Common 15-second structures:

- Pre-fight skill release: `0-3 对峙起势`, `3-7 能量汇聚`, `7-12 技能展开`, `12-15 威压定格`.
- 1V1 fight: `0-2 瞬发入战`, `2-5 近身缠斗`, `5-9 全屏对轰`, `9-12 破防反杀`, `12-15 绝杀定格`.
- 1V many fight: `0-2 合围破局`, `2-5 近身清场`, `5-9 全屏对轰`, `9-12 逐个击溃`, `12-15 全屏清场`.
- One-take transformation: `0-2 入场/坠地`, `2-4 凝视/意图`, `4-6 启动`, `6-9 裂变/附着`, `9-12 稳定/压制`, `12-15 终极战态/钩子`.

For non-action drama, do not use this action module structure. Keep exact 15-second ranges, but divide 文戏 into 2-3 second shot rows with speaker/reaction/detail/silence beats.

## Global S-Level Action Locks

- Strict 15 seconds per big storyboard.
- No empty shots, no flat frames, no low-energy filler.
- High-frame-rate cinematic motion, 8K-level clarity, rich depth, strong subject separation.
- Every second should carry action, pressure, reaction, atmosphere, or setup.
- Use Hollywood-level large camera movement only when it helps clarity and impact.
- Keep safety: no lowbrow exposure, no explicit injury detail, no readable text, no watermark/logo, no malformed bodies, no style drift.

## Action Construction

Every fight action needs:

1. `起势`: stance, foot plant, shoulder load, breath, eye target, weapon angle.
2. `发力`: waist/shoulder/wrist/finger power route.
3. `碰撞`: impact, block, dodge, clash, energy contact.
4. `物理反馈`: dust, cracked ground, sparks, cloth snap, wind ring, debris, recoil.
5. `对手反应`: stagger, guard break, retreat, howl, re-target, pressure shift.
6. `余波`: silence drop, smoke, particle fall, BGM beat, eye reaction.

## Motion Blur / Smear Frame for Impact

Use blur frames only where speed or impact needs to be felt. Do not write generic "motion blur everywhere". Every strike should be designed as `蓄力 → 出拳/挥击 → 击打 → 反馈`.

- `蓄力`: keep the body, weapon, and intent clear. Show source of force: lowered center of gravity, shoulder/waist loading, fist/weapon pulled back, breath held, foot planted. No blur in this phase.
- `出拳/挥击`: introduce controlled blur. Add jitter lines or smear along the actual motion direction. For higher speed, increase jitter amplitude and add speed lines trailing from the moving limb/weapon.
- `击打`: the impact frame is the decisive blur frame, but not always full-screen blur. If readability matters, keep the contact point readable and apply local blur only around fist/weapon/contact deformation, with a sharp impact anchor.
- `反馈`: complete the hit through the target's reaction. The opponent/prop must move, deform, recoil, slide, compress, or recover visibly. Add blur to the feedback frame only when the impact is heavy enough.
- `镜头震动`: use short, impact-synced camera shake only on collision or landing. Never keep the camera shaking continuously; constant shake weakens impact and makes the action unreadable.

Storyboard row wording should name the blur logic explicitly when used, e.g. `出拳段：右拳沿弧线产生方向性抖动线+短速度线`, `击中帧：拳面与沙袋接触点小面积模糊，沙袋轮廓保持可读`, `反馈帧：沙袋向后摆出12cm并带轻微拖影，0.2s后回弹`.

For traditional martial arts, blur follows body/weapon direction. For xianxia action, combine blur with energy trails, but keep a clear impact anchor so the audience knows what hit what.

## One-Take Action

Use one-take only for classified action climaxes: chase climax, 1vMany turning point, transformation, flying-combat turn, large power release, or emotional revelation during active combat. Do not use one-take for 文戏, dialogue confrontation, negotiation, confession, grief, investigation, or ordinary threat.

One-take does not mean vague continuous motion. It means one camera path with clear internal nodes:

- Start frame: where the body and camera begin.
- Key frames: every decisive impact, reveal, transformation, or power shift.
- End frame: the hook or next action position.

Use handheld breathing camera only when grounded in body stress. Use steadicam/orbit when the moment is ritual, awakening, or hero reveal.

## Enemy and Crowd Logic

- 1V many scenes need differentiated enemies: near melee, ranged, aerial, elite, support.
- Enemies must attack from clear directions; avoid random clutter.
- Every defeat or pushback needs physical feedback and enemy reaction.
- Group pressure should be readable in foreground/midground/background layers.

## Sound for Action

Include BGM and action sound in `声音设计`:

- Start: low-frequency rise, breath, cloth, foot plant.
- Clash: blade ring, dull impact, sparks, shield hit, air burst.
- Peak: percussion hit, bass drop, silence gap, sonic boom.
- Aftermath: dust fall, ringing ear, breath, debris, fading particles.

If there is dialogue inside action, the exact dialogue must appear before or alongside action sound in the same cell.
